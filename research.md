My research explores the intersection of political psychology, ideology, and institutional performance, with an emphasis on quantitative research methods and comparative survey data in Latin America.

---

## Working Papers & Dissertation Chapters

### 📄 Generalized Trust and Attitudes Toward Migration in Latin America
*(Under Revision)*  
Generalized trust significantly improves positive attitudes toward migration across LATAM, but primarily when mediated by community participation and political discussion. Civic cooperation reinforces generalized trust, fostering a deeper understanding of complex social issues.

### 📄 Ideology and Social Identity in Comparative Perspective
*(Working Paper / Dissertation Chapter)*  
An empirical investigation into how political identity and ideological self-placement interact under varying levels of institutional polarization, and how these factors shape attitudes toward migration across Latin America.

### 📄 Vulnerable Employment and Attitudes Toward Migration
*(Working Paper / Dissertation Chapter)*  
Informal labor influences how individuals perceive migration across all skill levels. This paper examines how structural unemployment and fragile labor market characteristics trigger economic anxieties and anti-immigrant attitudes.

### 📄 Globalization Backlash on Investor-State Dispute Settlement (ISDS)
*(Working Paper)*  
Using survival analysis, this project explores the relationship between the volume of international arbitration cases against a host country and the duration of investment treaties. The findings identify a structural breakpoint around 2016, marking a shift toward more frequent treaty reforms and terminations.

### 📄 Quantitative Electoral Data & Institutional Performance
*(Ongoing Project)*  
Applied statistical modeling and data processing focused on electoral administration, voter turnout dynamics, and demographic behavior.
