## Research & Working Papers

* **Generalized Trust and Attitudes Toward Migration in Latin America** *(Under Revision)*  
  Generalized trust significantly improves positive attitudes toward migration across LATAM, but primarily when mediated by community participation and political discussion. Civic cooperation reinforces generalized trust, fostering a deeper understanding of complex social issues.

* **Ideology and Social Identity in Comparative Perspective** *(Working Paper / Dissertation Chapter)*  
  An empirical investigation into how political identity and ideological self-placement interact under varying levels of institutional polarization, and how these factors shape attitudes toward migration across Latin America.

* **Vulnerable Employment and Attitudes Toward Migration** *(Working Paper / Dissertation Chapter)*  
  Informal labor influences how individuals perceive migration across all skill levels. This paper examines how structural unemployment and fragile labor market characteristics trigger economic anxieties and anti-immigrant attitudes.

* **Globalization Backlash on Investor-State Dispute Settlement (ISDS)** *(Working Paper)*  
  Using survival analysis, this project explores the relationship between the volume of international arbitration cases against a host country and the duration of investment treaties. The findings identify a structural breakpoint around 2016, marking a shift toward more frequent treaty reforms and terminations.

* **Quantitative Electoral Data & Institutional Performance** *(Ongoing Project)*  
  Applied statistical modeling and data processing focused on electoral administration, voter turnout dynamics, and demographic behavior.

## Data Science & Applied Projects

### 📊 [Rule of Law and Globalization in LATAM](https://mondhector.github.io/rule-of-law-globalization/)
An exploratory study on the relationship between economic globalization and the Rule of Law in Latin America (2019–2023).
* **Skills & Tools:** Python (Pandas), Quarto, Seaborn, Plotly, Virtual Environments. Handled time-series alignment for asynchronous institutional datasets and built interactive multivariate visualizations.
* **Key Finding:** Trade agreements like USMCA/T-MEC facilitate market globalization for Mexico despite regional variations in institutional quality.

### 📈 [Homicides & Vulnerable Populations Analysis (2024)](https://mondhector.github.io/homicidios2024/analisis_homicidios.html)
A spatial and time-series study on homicidal violence in Mexico, focusing on vulnerable demographics (Indigenous, Afro-Mexican, and migrant communities).
* **Skills & Tools:** R, Quarto, Tidyverse, `ggplot2`. INEGI survey microdata processing, temporal trend analysis, and geographic data visualization.
* **Key Finding:** Identified statistically significant upward trends in violence targeting Indigenous populations in southern Mexico over recent years.
